When Technion students are called to milu’im, they must drop everything.

It doesn’t matter if there’s an exam tomorrow, or a paper due next week. Israel’s safety is on the line — and their support is needed right away. .

The Technion established the Student Reservists Fund precisely for these brave students. And today, I hope you’ll become one of the 50 alumni to support students on campus through this Fund and meet our Friday deadline.

Students pausing their life and studies for reserve duty for a few days, weeks, or even months is just part of life in Israel and at the Technion. But that doesn’t make returning to campus easy.

You or a classmate might have experienced it firsthand: The Technion’s heavy course load makes it hard to catch up. And the emotional toll of service can be overwhelming.

Over the years, the Technion has found that the best way to help reservists close the academic gap upon their return is through intensive, personalized tutoring. On top of that, reservists also receive special examination procedures, free photocopies, and additional counseling support.

This kind of support is expensive — and I think you’ll agree with me that it’s the least we can do for those who put themselves on the front line for Israel’s defense. And hitting our 50 donor goal by Friday means we can help even more students coming back to the Technion after milu’im.

Please, show your appreciation for Israel’s student soldiers: Make your gift today.
