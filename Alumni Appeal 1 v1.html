Dear [[?xx[[S1:first_name]]xx::xxxx::Friend::[[S1:first_name]]]] Fellow Alumnus,

Like you, I am a proud graduate of the Technion. Having completed three degrees at the Technion, I can say without a doubt that the Technion was a critical factor in the success I enjoy today as I advance my research to help find a cure for cancer.

But the Technion is challenging. You know there are a lot of long nights and even longer exams that go into a Technion degree. And many of us — including myself — faced the dual challenge of putting aside our studies to serve our reserve duty.

Interrupting coursework to serve Israel could have derailed our degrees. But because of the Technion Student Reservists Fund, it didn’t. The Technion made sure we had the individual tutoring, study materials, and emotional support we needed as scholar-soldiers.

The next generation of student reservists need our help. ATS has set a goal of getting 50 alumni to contribute to the Technion Student Reservists Fund by Friday, and I want you to join me in making a gift.

When we were called to defend our country, it consumed our every thought. When we returned to campus we were welcomed with warm greetings and the much-needed assistance to rejoin our classes to continue to excel in our studies. It’s truly unique for such a rigorous institution to do so much to ensure the success of students resuming their studies.

This semester, approximately 500 Technion students will be called to regular reserve duty. They’ll serve an average of three weeks — that’s almost a month away from studies. But because of the Technion Student Reservists Fund, they’ll be able to seamlessly integrate back into academic life — and you can help them.

Please help me reach this 50 donor goal by Friday.

With so much uncertainty in the region, we as alumni must come together to provide vital services for these students and let them know they have our support. Thank you for ensuring the success of students returning from duty.

Sincerely,
